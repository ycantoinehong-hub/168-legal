# Privacy Policy

**Last updated: April 27, 2026**

168 ("the app") is a personal time-budget tool for iOS. This policy explains what data the app accesses, how it is stored, and what is shared with third parties.

The short version: **168 stores everything on your device. Nothing leaves your phone unless you explicitly connect a third-party service (e.g. Google Calendar) or enable cloud sync.**

---

## 1. Data we access

### Time entries you log
Manual entries you create — category, duration, optional note — are stored locally on your device.

### Apple Calendar
If you connect Apple Calendar, the app reads event start time, end time, title, and decline status to auto-classify events into your time budget. Events are stored locally on your device.

### Apple Health (HealthKit)
If you connect Apple Health, the app reads workout sessions and mindful sessions (start time, end time, type) to auto-classify them. The app does not write any data to Apple Health.

### Google Calendar
If you connect Google Calendar, the app reads event start time, end time, title, and decline status from your primary calendar. Authentication uses Google's OAuth 2.0 flow; the access token is stored securely on your device.

### Notifications
If you enable notifications, the app schedules **local** notifications on your device. No notification token is sent to any server.

---

## 2. Data we do NOT collect

- We do not run analytics or tracking SDKs.
- We do not collect device identifiers (IDFA, advertising IDs).
- We do not collect location data.
- We do not have user accounts or servers (as of this version).
- We do not sell, share, or use HealthKit data for advertising, marketing, or any purpose other than display within the app on your device.

---

## 3. Where data is stored

All data is stored **locally on your device** using iOS's standard storage. We do not operate servers and do not transmit your data to us.

If a future version adds optional cloud sync, this policy will be updated and you will be asked to opt in before any data leaves your device.

---

## 4. Third parties

The app integrates with the following third-party services **only when you explicitly connect them**:

- **Apple HealthKit** — system framework provided by Apple. Subject to [Apple's Privacy Policy](https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/).
- **Apple Calendar (EventKit)** — system framework provided by Apple.
- **Google Calendar API** — subject to [Google's Privacy Policy](https://policies.google.com/privacy). The app requests read-only access to your calendar via OAuth.

The app does not share data with any third-party service for advertising, analytics, or marketing.

---

## 5. HealthKit-specific terms

In compliance with Apple's HealthKit guidelines:

- HealthKit data is read on-device only.
- HealthKit data is **never** transmitted to our servers (we have none).
- HealthKit data is **never** shared with third parties.
- HealthKit data is **never** used for advertising, marketing, or data mining.
- You can revoke access at any time via iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Health.

---

## 6. Your rights

- **Delete data**: uninstalling the app removes all locally stored data.
- **Disconnect services**: in the app, go to Settings → Imports and disconnect any source. You can also choose to remove imported entries on disconnect.
- **Revoke permissions**: iOS Settings → Privacy & Security lets you revoke Calendar, Health, and Notifications permissions at any time.

---

## 7. Children

The app is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children.

---

## 8. Changes to this policy

If we materially change how data is handled, we will update this page and update the "Last updated" date above. Continued use after a change means you accept the new policy.
